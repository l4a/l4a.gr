The bohemian rhapsody effect

Filed under: Viewpoints

Have you ever seen Queen’s Bohemian Rhapsody video? In the very first shot the band members appear with a very narrow beam of light right above their heads. The result is a very dramatic image while hard shadows distort the characteristics of the faces. If this technique worked for Queen’s video believe me it doesn’t work for bath mirror lighting!

Usually in restaurants, offices, pubs, retail stores and even homes, you’ll find downlights in the ceiling right in front of the mirror. The result of this kind of installation is what i call “the Bohemian Rhapsody Effect”. The visual performance in front of the bathroom mirror is critical. The Lighting needs to be sufficient, uniform, glare-less and with no shadows. This can be achieved only with front diffused lighting (e.g. appliqués on both sides of the mirror, mirror built-in lights etc) and not with wrong positioned downlights.
