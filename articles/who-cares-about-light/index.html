Who cares about light?

Three or four years ago, I ran a poll for several months on the website of a company I was working at back then. I got some very interesting feedback for the question: “What is the biggest problem with lighting in your workplace, if any?”

Daylight Deficiency 32%
Glare from VDU’s or in the working plane 23%
Flat and monotonous lighting 16%
Artificial light levels too low in the working plane 11%
Light is too *cold* 9%
Annoying sunlight 4%
Artificial light levels too high in the working plane 4%
Light is too *warm* 2%

In a country like Greece with an average of almost 8 hours of sunshine per day, it was surprising that the majority of the 850 participants1 said that there wasn’t enough natural light in their workplace!

Urban sprawl, and the obsolete urban planning system with its narrow streets is, of course, an issue, but it is also a fact that architects in Greece do not take into consideration nature’s basic element, light. Believe it or not, it’s true. Only a small minority of people are aware of the importance of light integration. Can you imagine the tremendous energy savings that could be achieved by using daylight properly? Can you also imagine the benefits to people’s health and well-being?

I was speechless and quite disappointed when one of the best known architects in Greece told me, “I don’t care about light.” Correct me if I’m wrong, but what are the constituent elements of architecture? Aren’t they Matter and Light? Ignore one of them and there is no such thing as architecture. Saying “I don’t care about light” is like saying “I don’t care about architecture.” I can understand that nowadays architects must be knowledgeable in a huge spectrum of fields. I can understand if one doesn’t have the time to learn about air-conditioning, elevators or interior design, for example, but turning your back on light is like turning your back on the most useful tool for your profession.

Although our ancestors’ primary building material was light, nowadays its importance isn’t truly recognised here in this country with some of the most amazing sunlight in the whole world.

NV

  1. The poll was in greek only so it is safe to assume that the majority of the participants was greek (confirmed by Google Analytics stats). ↩
