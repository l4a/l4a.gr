Who cares about light?

Filed under: Daylighting, Viewpoints

Three or four years ago, at the website of the company that i was working then, I put a poll for several months. The feedback in the question “What is the biggest problem (in case you have one) regarding lighting in your workplace?” was very interesting.

Deficiency of daylight 32%
Glare in VDU’s or in the working plane 23%
Lighting is flat and monotonous 16%
Too low artificial light levels in working plane 11%
Light is too “cold” 9%
Annoying sunlight 4%
Too high artificial light levels in working plane 4%
Light is too “warm” 2%

The majority of the 850 participants1 said that natural light was not sufficient in their workplace in a country like Greece with an average of almost 8 hours of sunshine per day!

The sprawl, the obsolete urban planning system with the narrow streets, of course is an issue but it is a fact that architects in Greece do not take into consideration nature’s basic element, light. Believe it or not it’s true. Only a small minority is aware of the integration of light. Can you imagine the tremendous energy savings that could bring on the right use of daylight? Can you imagine also the benefits in people’s health and wellbeing?

I stood speechless (and felt very disappointed) when one of the best known architects in Greece told me “I don’t care about light.”. Correct me if I’m wrong, but what are the constitutive elements of architecture? Matter and Light. Without one of them there is no such thing as architecture. Saying “I don’t care about light” is like saying “I don’t care about architecture”. I can understand that the spectre of knowledge for an architect in our days is huge. I can understand if one does not have the time to learn about air conditioning, elevators or interior decoration for example, but turning your back on light is like turning your back on the most useful tool for your profession.

Although our ancestors’ primary building material was light, in our times it’s importance is not recognized here, in the place with the most amazing sunlight in the whole world.

NV

  1. The poll was in greek only so it is safe to assume that the majority of the participants was greek (confirmed by Google Analytics stats). ↩
